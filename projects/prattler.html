Brief: Redesign and build Pratt's student run school newspaper's, The Prattler, old outdated website to be more inline with their new branding.

The old Prattler is not something great to look at if we're being honest. it was built on tumblr and did not show any of the visual flair a publication like the Prattler should show.

When The Prattler approached me, they didn't have a lot to go on. At that time, they were still in the middle of creating their brand guideline, and the only visual they were able to give me was the new logomark.

While The Prattler worked on building out their brand guide, I began work on wireframing and creating user flows for the site. I simplified the navigation down to three root pages, so it would be easier for users to immediately jump into the site's content. It was ultimately designed responsively on a twelve column grid and a mobile breakpoint.

I wanted The Prattler's visual design to shine above the rest. While researching other publications' sites, I found a lot of similarity in them. A white background, very straightforward typesetting, etc. I wanted The Prattler to be loud and bold.

To bring the publication into the 21st century, I designed it with interactivity in mind. The Prattler should interact with the students on a daily basis, therefore students should be able to "interact" with The Pratter as well.

Using the depth from the prattler's logo, each article was made to jump out at the user, as if crying out to be clicked on. Like this, each interaction on the site is supposed to create user delight, and encourage them to explore the site to find more content and more interactions.
