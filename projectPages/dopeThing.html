gmessage



i've done a huge case study on this project! find it here on medium!



a slight preamble to the project: gmessage is project borne out of pure passion and utter frustration. i am not shy about my love for google, and i have always had a passion about making ubiquitous experiences (like messaging) easier and more fun to use. google's troubles in this space opened up a perfect opportunity for me. there's the passion. the frustration has come from my junior year of education at pratt institute. wanting to be an interactive designer, i've found pratt almost refuses to teach me and give me projects that i think are important to becoming well versed in the digital space. therefore, i created my own project -- not only to teach myself, but to show i know what i can do. directly below is the annotated deck of designs if you really want to pick my brain.

anyways, here's wonderwall.








stage 01:

identifying the problem. why haven't past google messaging services worked?

problem:
most users are hard set on their current messaging apps. getting them to switch can be a pain.

solution:
integrate the service into something everybody uses -- google chrome & the standard android messenger.

thought process: via user research, many people stated they used facebook's system simply because people are on facebook. by utilizing chrome, the web's most popular browser by a wide margin, we are beginning to integrate this messenger into a product people use on the daily.

stage 02:

wireframing the app. it is designed on an 8 dp grid with 16 dp margins.

after the initial ideation and brief user testing, i realised that while i was showcasing the most recent/important content up top, i was then making it the least reachable content on the screen. below are heat maps of areas that are hard to reach based off of two different hand positions, one where i found users held the phone in the middle, and one where they held from the bottom and propped it up with their pinky.

from this, i added the google assistant to give relevant info while keeping content in easy to reach areas for the user.

the messaging interface. for this i focused on the send bar. should the send button be inside or out? after a focus group, most people identified the send button making more sense outside of the send bar, as they prefer to keep attachments within the message as they see them as part of the message itself rather than a separate entity. this also works in highlighting send as an important cta when activated.

then comes the desktop. as stated before the desktop integrates directly into chrome, and acts as a slide out panel. as such, the design translates fairly straightforwardly due to the similar aspect ratios.

stage 03:

visual design. adding colour and branding to make the app feel fun as well as add user freedom with customizable colours.

within the visual design phase, messaging was built out even more to include group chats and sms versus standard cloud states.

some features might be slighter harder for a user to understand or naturally discover -- these will be explained concisely within the onboarding process. pinning will be explained fluidly after a small period of use. (onboarding illustrations done by the talented jooyoung park)

as stated before the desktop translation is fairly straightforward, and it holds closely to the original wireframes. you can see here its integration with chrome.

the entire app was built within a modular system, so colours and layouts can be changed easily at will. this of course allows for easy dark mode integration.











the final stage (i say final, but of course this was a circular design process) is prototyping. animations are incredibly important to the ultimate feel of the app. the standard messaging app uses system animations to go from state to state, and this ultimately breaks the experience.

that is gmessage -- the ultimate solution to google's messaging mess. be on the lookout within the coming weeks for the final updates to this project. this will include annotated wireframes, visuals, and prototypes, an entire case study with all my user and outside research, and some communications that would promote the app. thanks for reading.
